The important people

Director


Francis Lawrence

American director known for his music videos before taking over the Hunger Games saga from the 2nd film. He directed films 2, 3 and 4 of the saga.

Lead Actress


Jennifer Lawrence

American actress who plays Katniss Everdeen, the main character. She won an Oscar even before playing in Hunger Games. Her very natural acting made the character very believable.

The main characters


Katniss Everdeen

Personality: Brave, determined, protective. She always puts her family and loved ones before herself.

Special attribute: Bow and arrows — An exceptional huntress from district 12, her skill with the bow saves her life several times.

Peeta Mellark

Personality: Gentle, generous and diplomatic. He uses his words and charm rather than force to survive.

Special attribute: Camouflage — As a baker's son, he knows how to blend into his surroundings thanks to his mastery of painting and make-up.

Gale Hawthorne

Personality: Rebellious, passionate and idealistic. He is ready to sacrifice everything to overthrow the Capitol.

Special attribute: Trapper — Like Katniss, he illegally hunts to feed his family. He is also an outstanding military strategist.

The context and society

The story takes place in a dystopian future in North America, now called Panem. The country is divided into 12 districts (formerly 13), all under the domination of a totalitarian government located in the Capitol.


After a rebellion crushed 75 years ago, the Capitol established the Hunger Games: every year, each district must send one boy and one girl (called Tributes) between 12 and 18 years old to fight in an arena to the death. There can only be one survivor.


The districts live in misery and fear, while the Capitol enjoys extreme wealth. The Hunger Games serve to remind the districts of their powerlessness and to entertain the Capitol's population.

The Films

Film 1 : The Hunger Games (2012)

  • Katniss volunteers to replace her little sister Prim during the Reaping ceremony.
  • Peeta Mellark is drawn as the male tribute from district 12. Katniss remembers that he once threw her bread so she wouldn't starve.
  • The two tributes arrive at the Capitol and meet their mentor Haymitch, a former victor who is alcoholic but very intelligent.
  • During training, Katniss impresses the judges by shooting an arrow at their table. She receives a score of 11/12.
  • The Games begin: most of the tributes kill each other in the first few minutes near the Cornucopia.
  • Katniss survives by hiding in the trees and fleeing the strongest tributes, called the "Careers".
  • Rue, the young tribute from district 11, forms an alliance with Katniss. They sabotage the Careers' camp. Rue is killed and Katniss gives her a dignified death.
  • The rules change: two tributes from the same district can win together. Katniss finds Peeta, gravely injured.
  • Katniss and Peeta are the last two survivors. The Capitol cancels the rule change. They threaten to eat poisonous berries together. The Capitol gives in: they both win.
  • Haymitch warns Katniss that President Snow is furious: her gesture with the berries was an act of rebellion, not love.

Film 2 : Catching Fire (2013)

  • Katniss and Peeta go on their victory tour of the districts. Katniss sees signs of rebellion everywhere inspired by her berry gesture.
  • President Snow visits Katniss and orders her to convince Panem that her gesture was out of love, not revolt. Otherwise, she and her loved ones will die.
  • Snow announces the 75th edition of the Hunger Games, the "Quarter Quell": the tributes will be drawn from former victors. Katniss and Peeta are therefore forced to return.
  • In the arena, an alliance forms between Katniss, Peeta, Finnick (district 4), Beetee and Johanna to eliminate the most dangerous tributes.
  • The arena is shaped like a clock with danger zones that activate at specific hours (blood rain, toxic fog, muttation monkeys…).
  • Beetee attempts to destroy the arena using lightning and an electric wire.
  • Katniss understands Beetee's plan and uses her bow to send an electric wire into the arena's force generator. She destroys the protective dome.
  • Hovercrafts arrive and retrieve Katniss, Finnick and Beetee. Johanna and Peeta are captured by the Capitol.
  • Haymitch reveals to Katniss that the rebellion was planned from the start. A large part of the tributes were aware of it.
  • District 12 is bombed and destroyed by the Capitol. Katniss joins district 13, which still exists in secret.

Film 3 : Mockingjay (2014 & 2015)

  • Katniss arrives at district 13, underground and militarised. President Coin asks her to become the "Mockingjay", the official symbol of the rebellion.
  • Peeta appears on the Capitol's television, manipulated and conditioned to condemn the rebellion. Katniss agrees to be the Mockingjay in order to save him.
  • Katniss films propaganda spots (the "Propos") to galvanise the districts in revolt. Her authentic appearances in the field are far more effective than the prepared videos.
  • A commando mission is sent to the Capitol to free Peeta and the other prisoners. The mission succeeds, but Peeta has been conditioned to kill Katniss.
  • The districts one by one rise up and push back the Capitol's forces. The civil war spreads across all of Panem.
  • The rebel forces advance towards the Capitol through booby-trapped streets (the "pods"). Katniss's squad suffers heavy losses, including the death of Finnick.
  • Prim, Katniss's little sister, is killed in an explosion in the streets of the Capitol. Katniss is devastated.
  • Snow is captured. Katniss is designated to execute him at a public ceremony. She turns her bow against President Coin instead, killing her.
  • Snow dies shortly after, trampled by the crowd. Panem is liberated. Elections are organised.
  • Years later, Katniss and Peeta live in the rebuilt district 12. Katniss slowly accepts to live again, despite the nightmares and the grief over Prim.

My Opinion

What I liked


- The character of Katniss is very well written: she is not a perfect heroine, she is human and that is realistic because she doubts and she suffers.


- The universe of Panem is very coherent and well built. You clearly understand the Capitol's oppressive system.


- The critique of society (reality TV, propaganda, inequality) is intelligent and still relevant today.


- Jennifer Lawrence's acting, who is excellent in the lead role.

What I liked less


- The romance between Katniss, Peeta and Gale sometimes takes up too much space compared to the main story.


- Film 3 is split into two parts, which slows the pace down a lot. Part 1 is quite long.


- Some secondary characters could be better developed (Johanna and Finnick would deserve more screen time).

Key scene from Film 3

I am very proud that one of the most important scenes was filmed near where I live. It was shot at the Palacio, not far from the school, and the place attracted the director (Francis Lawrence) with its majestic and imposing atmosphere.

My favourite film: Catching Fire (Film 2)


It is the most balanced film of the three. The clock-shaped arena is very original, the alliances between tributes are surprising, and the final revelation (the rebellion organised from within) is truly unexpected. The pace is perfect between action and emotion, unlike film 3 which is too fragmented, and film 1 which lays a lot of groundwork. Film 2 is the one that kept me on the edge of my seat from start to finish.
