The game

This game is excellent.

It requires building a "deck" from 8 cards such as spells, buildings, air troops, etc.

You can then upgrade your cards up to level 16, although some start at level 3, 6, 9 or 11 depending on their rarity.

Finally, you can engage in battles against online opponents.

A match

Each match unfolds as follows:

Both players place cards for 3 minutes without interruption, unless one of them takes the king's tower.

After three minutes, the player who has taken the most towers wins the match.

If an equal number of towers has fallen on each side, the match is extended by 2 minutes and ends as soon as one player has more towers than the other.

After 5 minutes, if no winner has been declared, a special mechanic kicks in. The match stops and all towers lose health at the same rate.

Special cases

Two players can draw if both king's towers are taken at exactly the same time.

During the 2-minute overtime, if two towers fall at the same time, the match does not end.

During the sudden death phase, after 5 minutes, if two towers fall at the same time, the match does not end and the towers continue to lose health at the same rate.

What I like

Clash Royale offers great diversity in terms of cards you unlock along the way. But also in terms of game modes, some of which are seasonal. Moreover, some cards have evolutions making them more powerful, and others have hero versions. All these mechanics make the game even more interesting.

There are wildcards that you can obtain to upgrade cards. This makes them more powerful, and choosing which one to upgrade first is a strategic element that can make all the difference.

Players are divided into more than 20 arenas. This way, you cannot (normally) face a player who is much stronger or who has cards and towers of a significantly higher level.

There are several types of towers, including one that can take out small, numerous cards but is helpless against a tank. Another deals a lot of damage but attacks slowly. One is more balanced. And another slightly weaker than the balanced one but capable of boosting card levels during the match.

What I like less

Clash Royale, over the past few years, has been drifting toward the "pay to win" category. Indeed, since its beginnings, Clash Royale has always allowed players to buy gems, wildcards, evolutions, heroes... with real-world money. In that sense, Clash Royale used to fall into the "pay to fast" category — meaning that by paying, you have more fun and progress faster. But the more evolutions and heroes are created for cards, the more new levels become accessible (previously max level = 11, now max level = 16), and the more players who don't pay find themselves stuck in their progression or facing opponents with cards of much higher levels, breaking the game.

That said, Clash Royale is still a good game, but I am noticing more and more bugs and small malfunctions. It seems like SUPERCELL has abandoned the idea of making Clash Royale a fun game in favour of turning it into a revenue stream. This game has changed a lot since its beginnings, 10 years ago.
